about me.

i am a computer and electrical engineer by way of the university of virginia, a former microsoft program manager for windows server active directory, a current microsoft consultant for identity management products in the public and private sector, and an aspiring web developer. i am interested in the intersection of technology and social justice. over the past decade mobile technology has spread rapidly to parts of the world that were once isolated from the global exchange of data and information. their contributions to the internet have already had a profound affect on geopolitics. this impact will only continue to grow and i hope to contribute with web applications that harness the ease and ubiquity of mobile devices to spread literacy and rights especially for women.

travel.

london. summer 2005.

i have been pretty fortunate in my post-college years to have travelled extensively across the us and to different countries. the idea of travel and its implementation (after i made some cash) have always an important part of my life. travel forces me to reflect on my culture, privilege, morals, really anything that is subjective and open to evaluation. so given that, my very first trip outside of the us holds a very special place in my heart. the summer of freshman year i went to london with a friend. we were relatively poor college students and stayed with an aunt of mine in the suburbs of london. we visited all the usual tourist spots: big ben, the tower of london, the london eye, tate modern but what i love most was roaming the streets on my own and getting lost in the small alleyways, among buildings that were likely hundreds of years older than anything i had seen in the states. because we were over 18, we felt like grown ups drinking in pubs with londoners after work let out. we took a day trip to oxford; what an absolutely incredible city with brilliant people and gorgeous architecture - to this day i hope to somehow attend that school.
